tree-based sweep-testing collision system that can process moves and rotations efficiently

split into 3 layers: solids, bricks, and things:
- "solids" are stuff like platforms and moving platforms, they don't react to the game world
  and kind of just do their own thing unless they're pushing something on a lower layer
- "bricks" are like solids, but interact with solids and react to collisions; if a solid is pushing
  a brick in a way that'd crush it, the solid will actually stop its move and signal to itself to do
  some sort of correction system (so a moving platform potentially crushing something like
  the schooner will move the other way to prevent the crush)
- "things" are the "least respected" layer, since both bricks and solids can push them around.
  if a thing gets put into a crushing move, it's the *thing*'s job to tell the brick/solid to do
  something, or for itself to do something (which is likely how the crushing system will
  work during gameplay)
